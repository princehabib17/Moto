# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young motorcycle enthusiasts and riders aged 18–35. They browse on mobile and desktop, often for inspiration or to explore bikes. They're drawn to streetwear culture, hype drops, and cinematic visuals. They expect a site that feels as fast and alive as the machines it sells.

## Product Purpose

An interactive motorcycle showcase website with a wow factor. The site features a background video that animates on scroll, creating an immersive, cinema-quality experience. Success means every visitor feels something visceral the moment the page loads — speed, power, attitude.

## Brand Personality

Bold, cinematic, unapologetic. The brand doesn't sell motorcycles, it sells the feeling of riding one. Voice is direct, confident, minimal. Let the visuals do the talking.

## Anti-references

- Generic car/auto dealership sites: boring, corporate, template-feeling
- Typical AI-generated landing pages: cream backgrounds, identical card grids, eyebrow labels on every section, hero-metric templates
- Safe minimalism: Apple-clone cleanness with no edge, no attitude

## Design Principles

1. **Motion is the product.** Every scroll, every transition earns its place. The video and UI must feel choreographed, not assembled.
2. **Show, don't tell.** No marketing buzzwords. No "cutting-edge" or "seamless." The experience says it for us.
3. **Own the darkness.** This is a dark-mode-first brand. The UI lives in shadow so the bike and video can be the light source.
4. **Attitude over decoration.** Restraint where it counts, impact where it matters. No gratuitous effects — only those that amplify the machine.
5. **Scroll = story.** The page is a sequence. Each section is a beat in a narrative, not a grid of cards.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No strict WCAG level required. Prioritize visual impact. Ensure body text is legible (≥4.5:1 contrast) and all animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
